My bio

I am driven by a minimalist technological optimism: my pessimism for the future is offset by my dismay at the past.

To put it another way, I am profoundly concerned at the darker directions being taken by emerging technologies, but simultaneously excited about some of the possibilities they offer to restructure our world in ways that make it less unjust and more inclusive.

We have tools in our hands to redress the entrenched injustices of the past and to create new forms of communal existence, but most people seem to be using them exclusively to make more effective adversting. Unbelievably exciting technologies are being used to do the most boring and terrifying things imaginable.

I am particularly interested in AI (or automated decision making - ADM) as it impacts what it means to be human in today's world. By automating certain decisions, whether ethical or political, do we undermine fundamental aspects of our humanity?

I believe that philosophy, ethics, and human rights have an essential role to play in directing technological progress, so that we don't end up winning a technological arms race that equates to losing our humanity.
